### 1. Welcome & Purpose  

Welcome to the **R-Ladies Mentoring Program**! 💜  

This program was created to support chapter organizers in starting, reactivating, or strengthening their local communities.  
As a mentor, you will accompany a mentee (an R-Ladies organizer) by sharing your knowledge, experience, and encouragement to help them succeed in building a sustainable community.  

---

### 2. Who can be a Mentor  

All R-Ladies organizers with experience running a chapter are welcome to [**apply**](https://airtable.com/appqgHVVotuCP6aLy/pagJAHjC8DqlPzWqZ/form) as mentors.  

---

### 3. Your Role as a Mentor  

- Be supportive: listen actively and provide encouragement.  
- Share your experiences, including both successes and challenges.  
- Offer resources and strategies that have worked in your chapter.  
- Respect time and communication agreements with your mentee.  
- Remember: you don’t need to have all the answers, being a sounding board is valuable too!  

---

### 4. Before the Mentorship Starts  

- You will receive an introduction email connecting you with your mentee.  
- Reply to confirm your participation.  
- Read the attached **Mentor Guidelines** and the **R-Ladies Code of Conduct**.  
- Agree with your mentee on:  
  - Preferred communication platform (Slack, email, Zoom, etc.).  
  - Frequency of meetings. We suggest a realistic rhythm (for example, every two weeks or once a month) that you can both sustain, keeping in mind this is a voluntary commitment.  
  - Confidentiality preferences (yes/no).  

---

### 5. During the Mentorship  

Focus on supporting your mentee’s growth as a community organizer. Topics may include:  
- Organizing and promoting events.
- Recruiting and supporting co-organizers.  
- Engaging and sustaining the community.  
- Sharing R-Ladies resources and networks.  

💡 **Tips to make meetings effective:**  
- Encourage your mentee to prepare a short **agenda** with 2–3 topics or questions.  
- Share your own experiences and examples that may guide their situation.  
- At the end of each meeting, agree on **concrete next steps** together (1–2 specific tasks or next steps to complete before the next session). 
- Help your mentee keep tasks manageable and don't forget to **celebrate progress**🎉.

  💡 Example things you might share:  
- Templates or checklists you use in your chapter.  
- Stories about how you overcame a challenge.  
- Recommendations on tools, platforms, or strategies.

Some useful links you may want to share:
- [**Tips on recruiting co-organizers**](https://guide.rladies.org/organization/intro/co-organizers/)
- [**Funding opportunities**](https://guide.rladies.org/organization/intro/finace/) (recommend [**R Consortium grants**](https://guide.rladies.org/organization/intro/finance/#r-consortium-grants))
- [**Finding and recruting speakers**](https://guide.rladies.org/organization/events/speakers/)

---

### 6. Common Challenges  

- If communication isn’t flowing, suggest adjusting the frequency or platform.  
- If you feel the match isn’t a good fit, let the mentoring team know so we can reassign.  
- Everything shared in the mentorship is confidential unless both agree otherwise.  

---

### 7. Ending the Mentorship  

- The mentorship usually ends when your mentee feels ready or when their main concerns have been covered.  
- You’ll be asked to complete a short [**Feedback Form**](https://airtable.com/appqgHVVotuCP6aLy/pagWIwf4vWJl8IxcR/form) to help improve the program.  
- You are welcome (but not required) to stay in touch with your mentee afterwards.  

---

### 8. Where to Ask for Support  

- If you need help, contact the R-Ladies Global Team at **mentoring@rladies.org** or via Slack.  
- Remember: all interactions must follow the **R-Ladies Code of Conduct**.
