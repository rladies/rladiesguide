### 1. Welcome & Purpose  

Welcome to the **R-Ladies Mentoring Program**! 💜  

This program was created to support chapter organizers in starting, reactivating, or strengthening their local communities.  
As a mentee, you will be paired with an experienced organizer (your mentor) who can share advice, resources, and encouragement to help you succeed.  

---

### 2. Who can be a Mentee  

- Organizers from new chapters
- Organizers from inactive chapters
- Any organizer seeking support

All R-Ladies organizers are welcome to [**apply**](https://rladies.org/form/mentoring-signup/) if they believe the program could benefit their chapter.
Example: new organizers stepping into the leadership of an already active chapter.

---

### 3. Your Role as a Mentee  
- Take the lead in your own growth: bring questions and set goals.  
- Be proactive: schedule meetings and prepare topics to discuss.  
- Respect time and communication agreements.  
- Keep an open mind and be willing to learn from your mentor’s experiences.  

---

### 4. Before the Mentorship Starts  
- You will receive an introduction email connecting you with your mentor.  
- Please reply to confirm your interest in participating.  
- Read the attached **Mentee Guidelines** and the **R-Ladies Code of Conduct**.  
- Agree with your mentor on:  
  - Preferred platform of communication (Slack, email, Zoom, etc.)  
  - Frequency of meetings. We suggest choosing a realistic rhythm you can sustain over time (for example, every two weeks or once a month), keeping in mind that this is a voluntary commitment.
  - Confidentiality preferences (yes/no)  

---

### 5. During the Mentorship  
Use the time with your mentor to discuss practical aspects of running a chapter, such as:  
- Planning events.  
- Finding and supporting speakers.  
- Promoting your chapter.  
- Keeping your community engaged.  

💡 **Tips to make meetings productive:**  
- Prepare a short **agenda** before each meeting (2–3 topics or questions).  
- Share the agenda with your mentor in advance if possible.  
- At the end of each meeting, agree on 1–2 **specific tasks or next steps** to complete before the next session.  
- Keep tasks small and realistic to maintain momentum.  

💡 Example questions to bring to your mentor:  
- How do I promote my first meetup?  
- What are some engaging event formats?  
- How can I find new co-organizers?  

---

### 6. Common Challenges  
- If communication isn’t working, try adjusting the frequency or method.  
- If you feel the match isn’t right, let the mentoring team know, as reassignments are possible.  
- Everything shared in the mentorship is confidential unless both agree otherwise.  

---

### 7. Ending the Mentorship  
- The mentorship usually ends when you and your mentor feel you’ve covered the main checklist or when your chapter is active again.  
- You’ll be asked to complete a short [**Feedback Form**](https://airtable.com/appqgHVVotuCP6aLy/pagWIwf4vWJl8IxcR/form) to help improve the program.  
- You are welcome (but not required) to stay in touch with your mentor after the program.
